Puzzle Piece

Emotional Interaction & Accessibility

Role

UX/UI Designer

Timeline

3 Weeks

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Context

Academic Project

The Challenge

Many design projects focus on usability and function, but fewer explore quiet emotional moments — the kind that make people pause and feel a small sense of connection. The challenge was to create a simple visual piece that communicates alignment, recognition, and the idea of “the click” when something finally fits.

The Concept

"Puzzle Piece" is a visual metaphor exploring connection and emotional clarity. The two pieces represent alignment — the moment when something finally makes sense. The center text, “Piece 315 — THE CLICK,” symbolizes that feeling when things fall into place.

Final visual artwork

The QR Code

A QR code was included in the experience, originally linking to an accompanying visual. The code is now deactivated, but its presence remains as part of the original composition.

Original accompanying QR code (inactive)

The Result

“Puzzle Piece” became a calm, gentle visual exploration of emotional alignment. It doesn’t require interaction — its meaning comes from the feeling it creates. A simple, quiet design with a soft pastel palette and minimal composition.

Emotional Journey: Finch to Union — Line 1 TTC

AR & Public Space Design

The Concept

Commuting on Line 1 often feels stressful, overstimulating, and emotionally heavy. This project reimagines the TTC journey from Finch to Union as a soft emotional experience — mapping feelings instead of stops. Each station represents a shift in mood, energy, or personal awareness, capturing how a commute can quietly affect the inner emotional landscape of the rider.

Emotional Mapping

I translated the commute into a sequence of emotional states — calm, overstimulated, reflective, grounded — tied to specific stations. Instead of geographic distance, the map expresses emotional distance, showing how a rider gradually transitions from one feeling to the next as the train moves south toward Union Station.

Emotional map visualization from Finch to Union

Design Process

I focused on “calm technology” principles — designing visuals that feel gentle, slow-moving, and emotionally spacious. The colours shift subtly as the rider moves from station to station, reflecting changes in energy. The type is soft, minimal, and grounded to mirror the emotional rhythm of a quiet morning commute.

Early sketches and visual exploration of mood transitions

The Result

The final piece presents the TTC Line 1 commute as an emotional timeline rather than a physical route. It turns a familiar journey into a reflective, almost meditative experience — helping viewers recognize the emotional shifts that happen quietly during everyday travel. A soft, pastel-based visual language reinforces the feeling of calm connection throughout the journey.

Discover BC

Travel App UI

The Goal

Travel planning is often overwhelming — full of ads, urgency, crowded layouts, and stressful decision-making. Discover BC was designed as the opposite: a calm, scenic travel booking experience that prioritizes clarity, ease of navigation, and emotional spaciousness.

UI Strategy

I removed all “urgent” visual patterns: red badges, countdown timers, and flashing deals. Instead, the interface uses soft greens, warm neutrals, and open spacing to guide the user through the booking process. Typography is clean and grounded, offering a calm reading rhythm.

High-fidelity UI screens showcasing the calm visual direction

Design Process

I began with competitive research, identifying common stress points in popular travel apps: cluttered menus, aggressive promotions, and complex navigation. From there, I created a structure that emphasizes simplicity — clean cards, intuitive categories, and predictable flows.

Wireframes exploring calmer booking flows

Moodboard: BC landscapes, airy grids, neutral tones

Final Production

The final interface blends scenic imagery with guided travel planning. Each screen is structured to make the user feel oriented — not rushed. Buttons are spacious, labels are clear, and the colours reflect the natural calmness of BC’s landscapes.

Final mockups demonstrating the clean and calming booking experience

The Result

Discover BC offers users a clear, grounded way to explore British Columbia. It reduces cognitive load, replaces visual noise with emotional calmness, and frames travel planning as an enjoyable, open experience.
