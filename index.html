Emotional Journey: Finch to Union — Line 1 TTC

AR & Public Space Design

The Concept

The commute along Line 1 can feel overwhelming. This project reimagines the journey from Finch to Union as a soft emotional experience — mapping feelings instead of stops. Each station reflects shifting energy and internal states.

Emotional Mapping

Instead of geographic distance, the visualization measures emotional distance. Moods gradually transition — overstimulation, calm, reflection, grounding — as the train moves south.

Emotional map visualization

Process & Development

This project developed through emotional research, colour studies, and a Miro-based exploration of how TTC stations reflect internal states. Instead of traditional sketching or moodboards, the process focused on mapping feelings, transitions, and symbolic colour language inspired by both the TTC and the New York Vignelli subway system.

Emotional Colour Framework

My emotional mapping used a colour system inspired by the New York Vignelli subway map. Each emotion was assigned a line colour, helping express the internal “route” a commuter travels while moving from Finch to Union.

  • Yellow — Happiness / relief
  • Blue — Sadness / heaviness
  • Red — Anger / overwhelm
  • Black — Fear / anxiety
  • Purple — Surprise / awakening

Emotional colour references inspired by NYC Vignelli subway design.

Process Mapping in Miro

The main development work happened inside a single Miro board. Here, I mapped emotions to TTC stations, explored narrative pacing, and planned transitions for the Finch → Union journey.

Miro board used to plan emotional transitions and TTC station meaning.

Early Digital Layout Exploration

Instead of physical sketches, rough versions were created directly in digital format. These early layouts helped define pacing, spacing, and emotional transitions for the final piece.

Early digital variations showing emotional sequencing and TTC-inspired structure.

Station-by-Station Emotional Notes

Throughout the process, I documented how each station feels emotionally — not physically. These emotional cues guided the colour transitions and mood shifts in the final visual.

  • Finch → Sheppard — anticipation, quiet nerves
  • Sheppard → Eglinton — heaviness, routine, grey emotions
  • Eglinton → Bloor — emotional noise, crowd overwhelm
  • Bloor → Union — grounding, calm, soft return to self

The Result

The final piece reframes a familiar commute as a reflective, emotional timeline. A soft pastel palette conveys a sense of calm connection throughout the journey.

Discover BC

Travel App UI

The Goal

Travel planning is often stressful. Discover BC was designed as the opposite — a calm, scenic booking experience built for clarity, ease, and emotional spaciousness.

UI Strategy

The interface removes stressful design patterns (countdowns, red badges, clutter) and replaces them with gentle colours, open spacing, and grounded typography.

UI Screens

Design Process

After competitive research, I created cleaner flows to reduce decision fatigue. Wireframes focused on clarity, predictability, and emotional grounding.

Wireframes

Moodboard

Final Production

The final UI blends scenic imagery with a clear booking flow. Buttons are spacious, labels are readable, and visuals feel light and open.

Result

Discover BC reframes travel planning into a calm, intuitive journey. It reduces cognitive load and introduces emotional calmness through soft, spacious design.
