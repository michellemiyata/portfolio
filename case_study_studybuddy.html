About Production

Brief Description

StudyBuddy is an inclusive productivity web application designed to support neurodivergent students and those with diverse learning needs. It prioritizes accessibility through sensory-friendly tools, managing executive dysfunction, and supporting multi-modal learning styles.

Creative Problem

Standard productivity tools often ignore the cognitive barriers faced by students with ADHD, dyslexia, or sensory processing differences. Cluttered interfaces and lack of sensory control can increase cognitive load. The challenge was to create a "calm technology" solution that reduces overwhelm and promotes equitable access to study resources.

Solution

I developed a unified platform using a "glassmorphism" aesthetic with high-contrast elements for visibility. Key features target specific accessibility needs: Text-to-Speech (TTS) for reading support, Ambient Soundscapes for sensory regulation, and a Simplified UI to minimize distractions and aid executive function.

Key Features & Design

Dashboard & Overview

The central hub for tracking study sessions and vocabulary stats.

Focus Mode

A distraction-free environment designed for ADD/ADHD brains. Integrated "Brown Noise" and ambient soundscapes (Rain, Cafe) provide controlled sensory input (masking) to help regulate focus and block out distracting stimuli.

Smart Dictionary

Supports Dyslexia and Auditory Learners through integrated Text-to-Speech (TTS). Definitions are presented with clear hierarchy and spacing to reduce visual stress, making vocabulary acquisition more accessible.

Reflection

Outcome

StudyBuddy demonstrates how digital tools can be designed for inclusivity from the ground up. By prioritizing accessibility features—like sensory controls and multi-modal learning aids—the app empowers students with disabilities to manage their learning independently and with confidence.
